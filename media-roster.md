## SD Card Offload Schedule

> **Rule:** All cards must be offloaded at ConOps **every 3 hours** during convention hours.
> After offloading, the ConOps workstation syncs automatically to the **Nextcloud server**, where Reo (Philippines) can access files in near real-time.

### Card Rotation Strategy

Each shooter carries **two labelled cards**. Only **one card is active in the camera per offload window**. At offload time:

- Remove the active card from the camera → hand it to the ConOps operator for offload.
- Insert the swap card → return to shooting immediately with zero downtime.
- The offloaded card is returned to you once copying is complete and sync is confirmed.
- That card is now your swap card for the next window.

This keeps you in the field while the copy runs, and ensures only one card's worth of data is at risk at any given time.

> **Example:** Neon starts Day 1 with `NEON-A` in camera, `NEON-B` in pocket.
> At Offload 1 → hand `NEON-A` to ConOps, slot in `NEON-B`, keep shooting.
> At Offload 2 → `NEON-A` returned and confirmed synced → hand `NEON-B` to ConOps, slot `NEON-A` back in. Repeat.

### Offload Process (Standard — ConOps Station)

1. Remove the active card from your camera and hand it to the ConOps offload operator (or insert it yourself if self-serving).
2. Insert your **swap card** into the camera and resume shooting.
3. Insert the active card into the **ConOps SanDisk PRO-Reader** (permanently stationed on the offload workstation).
3. Copy all new **RAW files** to: `Nextcloud/FurDU2026/[NEON or JARROD]/[YYYY-MM-DD]/`
5. **Do not format the card** — wait until the Nextcloud sync status indicator confirms the upload is complete.
6. Log the offload in the ConOps offload sheet: time · shooter · card label · file count.
7. Return the card to the shooter. It is now their swap card until the next window.
8. The other shooter staggers their own offload by 10–15 minutes so someone is always in the field.

> **Note:** These are microSD cards. Use the SanDisk PRO-Reader — do NOT attempt to use a standard SD slot without a microSD adapter.

### Backup Offload Process (ConOps Station Down)

> Use this process if the ConOps workstation or its internet connection is unavailable. **Notify ConOps staff immediately when switching to backup mode** so they can work on restoring the primary station.

#### Required
- Jarrod's **SanDisk PRO-Reader** (field unit — already in Jarrod's kit bag)
- Either shooter's **personal laptop or phone** with the **Nextcloud mobile/desktop app** installed and logged in, OR a personal hotspot + browser access to Nextcloud
- A USB-A or USB-C port available on the personal device

#### Backup Steps

1. Take the active card to Jarrod's field PRO-Reader. Connect it to your own laptop via USB.
2. Open the Nextcloud desktop app (or browser upload at `nextcloud.[domain]`) — log in with your media team credentials.
3. Copy files into `Nextcloud/FurDU2026/[NEON or JARROD]/[YYYY-MM-DD]/BACKUP-OFFLOAD/`
   - Use the `BACKUP-OFFLOAD` sub-folder so Reo knows these arrived via the backup route.
4. If you have **no internet access** at all (hotspot also down):
   - Copy files to your **personal device's local storage** as a temporary hold.
   - Label the folder: `FURDU2026-[SHOOTER]-OFFLOAD[#]-PENDING`
   - Upload to Nextcloud at the earliest opportunity (next break, back at the hotel, etc.).
   - Notify Reo via Telegram that a pending upload exists and give an ETA.
5. Log the backup offload in the ConOps offload sheet (same fields + note: "BACKUP — ConOps down").
6. Do **not** format the card until the files are confirmed on Nextcloud, regardless of route.
7. Once the ConOps station is restored, do not re-upload — Reo will reconcile the `BACKUP-OFFLOAD` folder.

> **Tip:** Both Neon and Jarrod should install the Nextcloud app on their phones and confirm login credentials work **before the convention starts on Friday morning.**

## Key Highlights to Capture (Don't Miss These)

### Signature Moments
1. **Fursuit Walk beach group photo** — THE iconic shot of the entire convention. Get an elevated angle. Multiple exposures to ensure everyone's eyes (heads) are visible.
2. **Closing Ceremony charity total reveal** — The crowd reaction when the final number goes up. Have both cameras ready.
3. **Dance Competition / Floor Wars winning moments** — Freeze-frame the victory pose. Video the crowd eruption.
4. **Talent Show standout act** — You'll know it when you see it. Be ready to capture the crowd losing their minds.
5. **Aquaduck fursuiters on the water** — Unique, memorable, and cannot be recreated. Telephoto from shore.

### Atmosphere Shots (B-Roll Throughout)
- Fursuiters in the hallways between events — candid interactions, hugs, posing for fans
- The beach/ocean backdrop with any fursuiters outdoors
- Dealers Den bustle at peak time — the energy of the crowd browsing
- Artists actively creating at Artists Alley
- Late-night DJ vibes — lights, silhouettes, crowd dancing
- Convention badge close-ups, event signage, themed decorations
- First-timers' reactions (pair with My First Furcon panel)

### Consent & Sensitivity Notes
- Always respect "no photo" badges or lanyards if the convention uses them.
- For meetup/interest-group events (Puppy Play Workshop, Rubberfurs, Vore Meet, etc.) — **confirm with the event organiser before shooting** and check if any attendees wish to opt out.
- The Super Sponsor Dinner is a private event — get organiser approval first.
- When in doubt, ask first, shoot second.
