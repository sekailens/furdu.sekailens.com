## 1. Initial Interest / "Tell Me More"

> *Use when someone says they're curious, interested, or wants to know what's involved — before they've committed.*

---

Hey [name]! Thanks for reaching out 😊

Here's the short version of what being a media volunteer involves:

📸 **Role:** You'd be covering events throughout the con — panels, stage shows, meetups, the fursuit walk, and general con atmosphere. Stills photography is the main need, though video capability is a bonus.

🎫 **Media pass:** We issue media passes valid for the full 3-day con (Fri–Sun). If you weren't able to get regular rego, this gets you in the door specifically for coverage duties.

🎒 **Gear:** You'd need to bring your own camera and equipment — FurDU doesn't supply photography gear to volunteers. By "decent camera" we mean something with **manual exposure controls** (aperture, shutter speed, ISO) that can handle indoor low-light without everything going blurry or noisy — so a DSLR or mirrorless is ideal. A fast prime or versatile zoom lens helps a lot. We'll go into more detail if you want!

🕐 **Time commitment:** There's no expectation you're shooting 100% of the time. We work around your availability and assign you to specific events or time blocks, so you can still enjoy the con.

📱 **After the con:** You submit your raw/unedited photos to us and FurDU takes care of all the editing, culling, and publishing. You'd get credited for your work.

No formal experience needed — just a camera with manual controls, a willingness to help out, and a good attitude!

Want me to run you through the specifics, or are you happy to jump in? 🐾

## 2. "What Camera / Gear Do I Need?"

> *Use when someone asks about equipment requirements.*

---

Hey [name]! Just a heads up — **volunteers are expected to bring their own gear**. FurDU doesn't supply cameras or lenses to media team members, so you'd be shooting with your own kit throughout the con.

That said, here's exactly what we're looking for — and what we're not:

✅ **Great fit:**
- DSLR or mirrorless with interchangeable lenses
- A versatile zoom (e.g. 24–70mm, 18–135mm) or a fast prime (e.g. 50mm f/1.8)
- Camera can shoot in **Aperture Priority or full Manual mode**
- Handles **ISO 1600–3200** without the image becoming unusable (important for dim panel rooms and late-night DJ/dance events)
- Ideally capable of **continuous burst shooting** for action events (dance comp, Floor Wars)

✅ **Also works:**
- A fixed-lens compact or bridge camera with manual exposure controls and good low-light performance
- Sony/Canon/Nikon/Fuji prosumer cameras
- Older but capable gear is totally fine — a 10-year-old DSLR in good condition beats a brand-new phone

⚠️ **Harder to work with:**
- Phone cameras — fine for casual shots but we can't issue a media pass for phone-only coverage, and low-light/action performance usually isn't up to the job
- Fully auto point-and-shoots with no manual control
- Heavily entry-level gear where the shooter can't control shutter speed (motion blur on dancers is the main issue)

If you're unsure whether your kit is a good fit, just tell me what you're shooting with and I'll give you an honest answer 😊

## 5. "What Do I Do With My Photos After?"

> *Use when someone asks about photo submission, editing, credit, or what happens to the images.*

---

After the con, we ask volunteers to submit their raw/unedited photos — **FurDU handles all the culling, editing, and publishing**. No need to spend hours in Lightroom on your end!

Here's the general process:

1. Upload your **raw files** (RAW format — no JPEGs) to our shared folder (we'll give you the link)
2. Our media lead reviews and culls the best shots
3. FurDU edits and incorporates them into the official FurDU 2026 gallery and socials

📸 **Credit:** All published photos are credited to you by name (or handle if you prefer).

🕐 **Deadline:** We ask for selects within **7 days** of the con wrapping up (by ~26 April).

No pressure on quantity — even 20–30 strong shots from your coverage blocks is plenty!
